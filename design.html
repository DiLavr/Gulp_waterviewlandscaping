Landscape Design by Waterview Landscaping

Landscape Design begins with the creation of a unique design layout for your home tailored to your lifestyle and the style of your home. Limited only by our imagination…..using manmade and natural components we can create cozy, beautiful spaces defined by texture and color.

Beginning with a clean open structure and detailed with gardens flowing through and into open lawn terraces, tranquil ponds, sunken gardens, outdoor fireplaces, or fragrance gardens, Waterview Landscaping can create a custom design to suit your style.

Let's get acquainted

My name is Brenda Barclay.
I joined Waterview Landscaping in March 2020, As the Head of Landscape Design and Sales. I look forward to meeting and walking through your property with you.
For nearly 34 years , I managed extensive high-end projects for some of the most successful firms in the Hamptons, Long Island. After completing my Bachelor’s Degree in Ornamental Horticulture and Landscape Design in 1982, I earned my Master’s Degree in Landscape Architecture in 1986.
From there I began my career in high-end residential landscape design.

Before beginning any project I like to get to know the client. Understanding your client and how they use their property helps in the creation of a space which is appropriate and enjoyable for their family specifically. I believe the most important aspect of the design process is to listen to your client. By reviewing all aspects of the development of the property with the client ultimately the design will represent the client’s lifestyle.

Landscape Master Plans

The most effective way to begin the design process is through the creation of a Landscape Master Plan. The Master Plan gives us the opportunity to present comprehensive design ideas in a manner that the client can visualize. The homeowner then has a “road map” to prioritize and follow at their own pace.
As you can see in the portfolio below the designs are drawn to scale and depict all details of both the hardscape (pools, terraces, tennis courts, etc.) as well as the lawn and planting beds. Once all revisions have been completed the drawing can be used as a tool to estimate the project. Also it can follow the crews into the field so there are no misunderstandings between the design office, the client, and the crews building the project.
For large or smaller projects a design is the best way to proceed.
